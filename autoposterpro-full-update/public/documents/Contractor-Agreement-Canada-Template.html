RECITALS

WHEREAS, the Company is engaged in the business of providing software solutions for automotive dealerships, including the AutoPosterPro software extension for vehicle listing automation;

WHEREAS, the Contractor desires to provide sales and business development services to the Company as an independent contractor;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants herein, the parties agree as follows:

1. ENGAGEMENT AND RELATIONSHIP

1.1 Independent Contractor Status. The Contractor is engaged as an INDEPENDENT CONTRACTOR, NOT an employee. Nothing in this Agreement shall create an employer-employee relationship.

1.2 No Employee Benefits. The Contractor shall NOT be entitled to any employee benefits, including health insurance, vacation pay, sick leave, pension contributions, CPP employer contributions, EI premiums, or Workers' Compensation coverage.

1.3 Tax Obligations. The Contractor is solely responsible for all income taxes, GST/HST remittances (if applicable), CPP self-employment contributions, and any other taxes. The Company will issue a T4A slip for payments made.

2. POSITION AND SERVICES

Position: Field Sales Contractor

Services: Identify and contact potential automotive dealership clients; present and demonstrate Company software products; negotiate and close sales agreements; maintain accurate sales records; meet performance metrics.

Location: Remote (work from any location)

3. PROBATIONARY PERIOD

The first NINETY (90) DAYS shall constitute a probationary period during which either party may terminate with seven (7) days written notice, with or without cause.

4. COMPENSATION

Commission Rate: [____]% of all Setup Fees collected from customers acquired by the Contractor.

Enter commission percentage: 30%, 40%, 50%, or other as agreed

5. EXPENSES AND EQUIPMENT

The Contractor is SOLELY RESPONSIBLE for ALL costs and expenses, including:

(a) Travel, transportation, meals, and accommodation;

(b) Office space, utilities, and supplies;

(c) Telephone, internet, and communication expenses;

(d) Marketing materials not provided by the Company;

(e) Insurance (liability, vehicle, health);

(f) ALL TECHNOLOGY, HARDWARE, AND SOFTWARE necessary to perform services.

6. CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

"Confidential Information" includes: customer lists, contact information, purchasing histories, prospective customer lists, pricing strategies, software source code, algorithms, technical specifications, business plans, financial information, and any information marked as confidential.

The Contractor shall:

(a) Hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence;

(b) NOT disclose to any third party without prior written consent;

(c) NOT copy, photograph, screenshot, print, or otherwise reproduce any customer lists or databases;

(d) Return or destroy all Confidential Information upon termination.

Duration: These obligations survive termination for SIX (6) YEARS.

7. NON-COMPETITION AND NON-SOLICITATION

Non-Solicitation of Clients (6 Years): During the term and for SIX (6) YEARS following termination, the Contractor shall NOT directly or indirectly solicit, contact, or do business with any customer or prospective customer of the Company.

Non-Solicitation of Personnel (2 Years): For TWO (2) YEARS following termination, the Contractor shall not solicit, recruit, or hire any employee or contractor of the Company.

Non-Competition (2 Years): For TWO (2) YEARS following termination, the Contractor shall NOT engage in any business that directly competes with the Company's software products.

8. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

All intellectual property, including software, code, algorithms, trade secrets, trademarks, and copyrighted materials, are and shall remain the exclusive property of the Company.

The Contractor shall NOT reverse engineer, decompile, or attempt to derive source code from any Company software.

9. INDEMNIFICATION AND LIABILITY

THE COMPANY SHALL NOT BE LIABLE FOR ANY ACTIONS, CONDUCT, REPRESENTATIONS, OR OMISSIONS OF THE CONTRACTOR.

The Contractor shall INDEMNIFY, DEFEND, and HOLD HARMLESS the Company from any claims, damages, losses, costs, and expenses arising from:

(a) The Contractor's performance or conduct;

(b) Any breach of this Agreement;

(c) Any negligent or unlawful act by the Contractor;

(d) Any misrepresentation by the Contractor to clients;

(e) Any violation of applicable laws.

10. TERMINATION

For Convenience: After the probationary period, either party may terminate with thirty (30) days written notice.

For Cause: The Company may terminate immediately if the Contractor breaches any material term, engages in misconduct or dishonesty, violates any law, or breaches confidentiality obligations.

Effect: Upon termination, the Contractor shall return all Company property and Confidential Information. Commission is payable only on payments actually received prior to termination.

11. GOVERNING LAW

This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the Province of British Columbia and the federal laws of Canada. Any dispute shall be resolved by binding arbitration in Vancouver, British Columbia.

Amendment: May only be amended in writing signed by both parties.
